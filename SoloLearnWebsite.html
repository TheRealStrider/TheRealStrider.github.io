HTML5

Paragraphs

The paragraph feature lets you write text and have it formatted in a paragraph like manner. In the paragraph, you can use different tags to emphesize your words such as making them bold or italisized. You can even change the color of words to say red or blue using the font tag <font></font> and the color attribute. Another you can do is provide links to other webpages or websites such as this link. The last thing I will talk about for paragraphs is the alignment of them. By using the align attribute you can align your paragraphs to the left, center, or right.

Images

Images can be inserted using the image tag <img> and the attribute src. Further modification can be done by adding attributes such as height, width, and alt. The alt tag is only needed for those who are unable to see the image such as people who have poor internet connections or use a text to speach device.

Lists

Ordered Lists

  1. Ordered lists use numbers to show different items
  2. The tag is <ol></ol>
  3. To add items you must use the tag <li></li>
  4. Use this for when you are listing steps for something or listing items from greater to least or least to greatest.

Unordered Lists

  • Unordered lists use bullet points to list items.
  • The tag is <ul></ul>
  • Adding items is the same as ordered lists
  • Use this for when you just want to lists items and do't care for the order they go in.

Descripted Lists

Tags
Uses the <dl></dl> tag for starting the list.
Items
Uses <dt></dt> to add terms for items and <dd></dd> to add detail to the term.

Nested Lists

  1. Nested lists are lists with other lists in them.
    • Can be with a ordered or unordered list.
    • Have to put other list inside the <li></li> tag where you want them to go.
  2. Nested lists are not lists that use several lists put next to each other.

Tables

Tables are tables. Tables are simply just text put under or next to each other.
Tables are used to sort data like in Microsoft Excel. Tables can have background colors
