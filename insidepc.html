PSU (Power Supply)

The power supply or PSU for short is what gives the PC power.
It converts the AC voltage from an outlet into DC voltage.
A PSU takes the DC voltage and splits it into +3.3V, +5V, and +12V.
PSUs come in many different power limits, form factors, efficiencies, and how modular they are.

CPU (Processor)

The processor or CPU for short, is the brain of a PC.
It tells all the components what needs to be done and when it needs to be done.
There are two vendors for CPUs and they are Intel and AMD.
Both have different generations and sockets and must not be mixed with each other.
Pick what CPU you want first then choose the motherboard!

Storage

In order to boot up into an OS, your PC needs a storage device.
There are two types of storage devices that are put into the PC and not outside like a usb or external drive.
They are an SSD and HDD.
Differences for each type are as follows:

  • SSD
    • Connection
      • SATA
      • PCIe
      • M.2
      • U.2
      • mSATA
      • SATA Express
    • Speed (With DRAM chip)
      • 500MB/s - 3,940MB/s
    • Capacity
      • 120GB - 8TB
  • HDD
    • Connection
      • SATA
    • Speed
      • 5,400RPM
      • 7,200RPM (Common)
      • 10,000RPM
    • Capacity
      • 20GB - 20TB
